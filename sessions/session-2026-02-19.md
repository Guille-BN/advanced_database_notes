## What I understood
- Inner join, joins the records on both tables when joining.
- Left join, joins the records on the first table & the records on the second table that do not exist on the first table.
- Right join, joins the records on the second table & the records on the first tabñe that do not exist on the second table.
- Full join, joins all records on both tables.
- Cross join, joins each record on the first table with each record on the second table (almost never used in practice).
- We can use aliases when joining.
- Self join, can join tables with themselves, useful when selecting only certain data to join with certain other data.
